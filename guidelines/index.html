FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Mila Biology + AI Reading Group, its activities, and how you can get involved.

Who can join the Mila Biology + AI Reading Group?

Anyone interested in the intersection of biology and AI can join, including PhD students, post-docs, principal investigators, and industry professionals.

What topics are covered in the reading group?

We focus on machine learning applications in bioinformatics, as well as related areas in ecology, evolutionary biology, conservation biology, physiology, and more.

How can I become a speaker?

Any member of the reading group, from PhD students to industry professionals, is welcome to present their work or discuss a paper of interest.

How often do the meetings occur?

We meet once every two weeks, usually from 3-4 pm, both virtually via Zoom and in-person at Mila.

What is expected from participants?

Participants are encouraged to read the selected papers beforehand and come prepared with observations and comments for discussion.

How are meetings conducted?

Meetings are conducted in a hybrid format with presentations followed by discussions, fostering collaboration and knowledge sharing.
