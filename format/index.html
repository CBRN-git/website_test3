Description

The Mila Biology + AI Reading Group delves into research papers that sit at the intersection of biology and artificial intelligence. While our primary focus is on machine learning applications in bioinformatics, we also welcome machine learning-related studies across various biological disciplines, including ecology, evolutionary biology, conservation biology, physiology, and more.

Our group is open to anyone engaged in or interested in tackling problems that bridge these fields. Expertise in both biology and machine learning is advantageous but not mandatory. We strive to create a welcoming environment where members can connect, share insights, and foster meaningful collaborations.

Please note that our focus generally excludes medical and neuroscience-related papers. For these topics, refer to our dedicated Medical Reading Group and Neural-AI Reading Group.

When and How We Meet

Hybrid Meetings: We conduct our sessions both virtually via GoogleMeet/Zoom and in-person at Mila.

Our group convenes biweekly, typically from 2:00 PM and 3:00 PM ET (Montreal time). We kindly ask participants to be punctual and arrive a few minutes early to ensure a timely start. Respect for the speaker and fellow attendees is paramount, mirroring the etiquette of in-person presentations.

For specific meeting dates and times, please refer to our published Talks page.

Meeting Format

Option A: Paper Presentation

A group member will present a selected research paper from the literature (approximately 35-45 minutes). This will be followed by an open discussion, allowing attendees to share insights, ask questions, and explore the paper's implications.

Option B: Speaker Presentation

A guest speaker will present their own research work to the group. Presentations are flexible in length, up to a maximum of 50 minutes, and will be followed by a comprehensive discussion. This format provides an opportunity for speakers to showcase their work and receive feedback from peers.

In both cases, the speaker may choose to present in a discussion-style fashion (questions during the presentation) or to save questions for the end. We encourage all attendees to read the assigned paper before the meeting. Both presenters and participants should come prepared with observations, questions, and comments to enrich the discussion and facilitate a deeper understanding of the material.

Who Can Be a Speaker

PhD Students

PhD students bring fresh perspectives and cutting-edge research insights, enriching our discussions with their ongoing work and innovative ideas.

Post-Doctoral Researchers

Post-doctoral researchers offer advanced expertise and innovative approaches to challenges at the intersection of biology and AI, fostering deeper exploration and understanding.

Principal Investigators

Principal Investigators share their extensive experience and leadership in the fields of biology and AI, guiding conversations and inspiring collaborative efforts.

Industry Professionals

Industry professionals provide practical applications and real-world solutions, bridging the gap between academic research and industry needs in the realm of biological AI.
